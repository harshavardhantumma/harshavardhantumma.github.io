05

Roadway Infrastructure Economic Resilience — Capital Costing

A group capital costing study comparing HMA vs. PCC pavement alternatives for the City of Charlotte using NPV, sensitivity analysis, and scenario-based evaluation over a 35-year study life.

Capital Costing NPV Analysis Pavement Engineering Sensitivity Analysis Group Project In Progress
Problem & Objective

Current pavement selection decisions fail to evaluate HMA vs. PCC as long-term systems with external economic interactions. This project quantifies 35-year life cycle costs including initial costs, maintenance, major repairs, and residual value — using NPV at a 4.1% MARR to support Charlotte's infrastructure planning decisions.

Methods Used

Net Present Value (NPV) analysis comparing HMA (35-year life) vs. PCC (40-year life). One-at-a-time and two-at-a-time sensitivity analyses to identify key cost drivers. Scenario-based analysis across high, average, and low traffic loading to evaluate rehabilitation frequency and total cost under uncertainty.

Key Finding

HMA shows lower NPV cost savings of $14.59/SY over PCC under base case assumptions. The decision is most sensitive to PCC and HMA initial costs. When HMA initial and maintenance costs increase together, the savings decrease and the decision can reverse in favour of PCC — making scenario analysis critical for resilient infrastructure planning.

How AI Shook the World in 2025 and What Comes Next — CNN

How AI dramatically reshaped society in 2025 — from daily life and the economy to government policy and national strategy...

Main Findings

AI transitioned from a background technology into a dominant force influencing daily life, the economy, and government policy in 2025. Week 03

Artificial General Intelligence

Artificial General Intelligence — Risks and Governance

Exploring the risks, governance challenges, and societal implications of AI capable of performing tasks at or beyond human-level intelligence...

Main Findings

AGI is defined as AI systems capable of performing a wide range of cognitive tasks at or beyond human-level intelligence. Progress toward AGI introduces systemic risks including loss of human control, goal misalignment, and autonomous decision-making with harmful consequences. Current governance and safety frameworks are insufficient, and AGI could amplify threats like cyberattacks, misinformation, and economic disruption at unprecedented scale.

Broader Implications for Society

AGI could significantly disrupt labor markets by automating complex cognitive work, potentially leading to widespread job displacement and economic inequality. Concentration of AGI development within a small number of organizations may increase dangerous power imbalances. Societal trust in information systems may erode due to AGI-driven misinformation and deepfakes. The article stresses the need for global governance and interdisciplinary oversight, framing AGI as an existential societal challenge — not just a technology issue.

Relevance to Construction

AGI could transform construction through fully autonomous decision-making across design, scheduling, cost estimation, and risk management. However, adoption raises serious concerns about workforce displacement, ethical responsibility, and accountability for autonomous decisions in safety-critical construction environments — making robust governance frameworks essential.

Week 10

Vibe Coding

The Uncomfortable Truth About Vibe Coding — Red Hat Developer

Building software by conversing with AI rather than writing code manually — exciting but potentially dangerous without proper structure...

Main Findings

Vibe coding — building software by conversing with AI — is simultaneously the most exciting and most dangerous development practice to emerge in years. The core problem is "intent loss": when AI writes the code, the reasoning behind it is never documented, making it nearly impossible to maintain over time. Spec-driven development is introduced as a structured alternative, guiding AI to produce accurate, maintainable code. The real skill is knowing exactly what you want and expressing it clearly.

Broader Implications for Society

Non-developers are now shipping full applications, democratizing innovation but introducing serious risks — poorly structured AI-generated code deployed at scale could lead to security vulnerabilities and system failures. This raises important questions about accountability, digital literacy, and workforce readiness. Society must grapple with whether traditional coding skills are still worth developing as AI increasingly writes software.

Relevance to Construction

Construction firms rely on custom software for scheduling, budgeting, and BIM. Vibe coding could allow site managers without coding backgrounds to build internal tools faster — but with hidden risks if the underlying code is fragile. This is especially critical for digital twin and IoT integrations managing sensors, drones, and real-time data pipelines on smart construction sites.

A UN report warns that rapid AI adoption could trigger a “Great Divergence” — worsening inequality between wealthy and developing nations...

Main Findings

The UN report warns that AI could trigger a "Great Divergence" between wealthy and developing communities. The core argument is that AI deployment should be "people first" rather than "technology first," ensuring communities lacking basic infrastructure are not permanently left behind. Broader Implications for Society

AI promises significant public value through faster medical diagnoses, improved weather forecasting, and better analysis of health and poverty risks. Relevance to Construction

The exponential growth of AI infrastructure creates a surge in demand for advanced civil engineering to design structures supporting extreme electrical and cooling loads. Sophisticated facility management will be crucial to sustainably operate these complex buildings. However, smaller construction firms lacking digital tools could be severely outpaced by larger tech-enabled corporations, mirroring the global inequality the UN report warns about.

Atomic Habits
by James Clear
★★★★★

Tiny changes, remarkable results — a practical guide to building good habits and breaking bad ones.

Summary

Atomic Habits explores how tiny, consistent changes compound into remarkable results over time. James Clear argues that it is not the size of the goal that matters, but the systems and daily habits you build to get there. The book introduces the Four Laws of Behaviour Change as a practical framework for building lasting habits.

Connection to My Field

As a construction engineer, this book reinforced the importance of building systematic processes on job sites and in project management. Small improvements in workflow efficiency, safety checks, and team communication — done consistently — lead to major gains over time. The concept of systems over goals directly applies to how construction projects should be managed.

My Takeaway

This is one of the most practical books I have read. The idea of making habits 1% better every day is something I now apply to my academic work and personal routines. I would strongly recommend it to any engineering student looking to build a stronger work ethic and sustainable productivity habits.

What the rich teach their kids about money — a challenge to conventional thinking about wealth and financial independence.
